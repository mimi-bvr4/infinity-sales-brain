## SALES PIPELINE & HUBSPOT WORKFLOW

### Pipeline Structure
**Lead Pipeline →** (Sales) **Deal Pipeline →** (Event Team) **Deal Pipeline**

### Lead Stages
New → Attempting → Response Received → Initial Phone Call Scheduled → Initial Phone Call Completed → Tour Scheduled → Tour Completed / No Tour Needs Contract → Inactive

### Deal Stages
Tour Completed → No Tour / Needs Contract → Contract Sent → Contract Signed → Event Assigned to Event Director → Inactive

### Lead Flow
1. Lead comes in (Website Forms, WeddingWire, The Knot, Zola, HCTG, WedSociety, Google Ad Forms)
2. HubSpot updates contact properties (email, phone, venue, event type, guest count, event date, partner's name, budget)
3. HubSpot assigns Contact Owner based on venue
4. HubSpot checks for open Lead/Deal
   - If open Lead exists → adds additional venue, creates task for follow-up
   - If no open Lead → creates Lead, copies contact properties, enrolls in Initial Email Sequence (8 emails over 30 days), moves to "Attempting," sends text
5. **CRITICAL: Never change the Venue or Event Type of a Lead**

### Key Automations
- Lead replies to email → unenrolled from sequence → stage = "Response Received"
- Lead calls/answers → unenrolled → you change to "Initial Phone Call Completed" → task to send Post Call Email
- Lead books tour via Calendly → unenrolled → stage = "Tour Scheduled" → task for Tour Confirmation Email
- Tour completed → you move to "Tour Completed" → HubSpot creates Deal → tasks for After Tour Email and Send Contract from Bento
- No activity for 5 days (Lead) or 3 days (Deal) → task to follow up
- No activity for 30 days → Lead moves to "Inactive," lost reason = "Ghosted," enrolled in cross-marketing campaign

### Lead Management Rules
- Every new lead gets a text, call, and email (email is automated)
- Response within 24 hours during business hours (Tues-Sat 9-5)
- Follow up every 48 hours of no new activity
- If Electronic Signature viewed for contract sent over 48 hours ago → CALL CLIENT

## TOUR FRAMEWORK

### Discovery Questions (Ask Before/During Tour)
1. Ceremony and reception here?
2. How many people are you inviting?
3. Time of year? Flexible? Day of week preferences?
4. What other venues have interested you? Toured any yet?
5. Any must-haves or absolutely-nots?
6. What is most important to you about your big day?
7. Are guests mostly local or traveling?
8. Are we your first tour?
9. What's the budget? (Only ask once ON the tour — never before)

### Tour Marks (Hit Every One)
- Catering: Full-service on-site kitchen, all food prepped and served fresh, 75-page catering menu, custom menus available
- Bar: Full open bar included with Wedding Experience, all alcohol provided
- Staffing: Full event staff included in labor fee

### Personal Connection (CRITICAL)
Ask personal questions throughout: Where were you engaged? How did you meet? Who's the spreadsheet master? What are you most looking forward to? Any cultural elements? How do you want guests to describe your wedding when they leave?

### Closing Script
1. "I'm going to send you a Venue Rental Agreement for X date."
2. **Create Urgency:** "All dates are first come, first served. We do not hold dates. Please act quickly to secure your preferred date!"
3. **Gifts:** Custom glasses to toast with on wedding date and anniversary
4. **Call to Action:** Give at least one task ("finalize that date," "talk to mom and dad and let me know")
5. For 100K+ budget or repeat clients: Mention Infinity can provide full-service planning at ANY venue
6. Follow-up email must include personal details from the tour

## CLIENT FAQ ANSWERS (KEY RESPONSES)

**Can I bring my own caterer?** Yes, for a fee of $50/guest based on final guest count. It's never more cost effective. We accommodate outside caterers for religious/ethnic needs — Culinary Director will discuss options.

**Is there an F&B minimum?** No. Never. No minimums for any vendor service at any venue. You pay for exactly what you want.

**Do you have preferred vendors?** We don't tell you a specific vendor is correct for you. We find the CORRECT vendor for each service based on the individual client. We also work with vendors that have a special connection to the client.

**What is production?** Lighting and sound for your event. Yes, you need it. Ceremony audio + entertainment + lighting for event design. Nashville Lighting & Production provides a technician during your event.

**How does the planning work?** Full-service team (Event Director, Event Designer, Event Coordinator). 6 main planning meetings: (1) Initial Meeting, (2) Design Meeting, (3) Vendor Meetings, (4) Selections/Budget Review, (5) Culinary Tasting, (6) Final Meeting. Initial meeting ~30-45 days after booking. All meetings can be virtual except tasting. No limit to communication. Your team handles everything from booking through load-out.

**Do you allow pets?** Yes for ceremony, not for reception. Event team coordinates pet logistics after ceremony.

**What about parking?**
- SWF: Drop-off area + endless parking a short walk away
- TBB: R Lot of Stadium Parking when available; most guests Uber; valet lot can be rented
- TBT: Endless nearby parking (garages, Music City Center); most guests walk/Uber/shuttle
- ECD: Ample parking for all guests

**How much is a wedding?** No "normal" pricing — varies greatly based on selections. We provide a proposal with exact costs for Infinity-provided services. No minimum/starting costs.

**I don't want/need a planner.** Why? If to save money — no service fees, so no savings. If for creative control — you still have complete control; the team executes YOUR vision.

**How much is the labor fee?** Located in your contract. Based on guest count and length of event. Covers: day-of coordination, food service, bartenders, culinary staff, set-up, tear-down, delivery, cleaning, flip fees, private tasting staff. One transparent fee vs. multiple hidden fees at other venues.

**What's the alcohol policy?** All alcoholic beverages purchased from Infinity Hospitality. Culinary Director works with you on selections and experiences.

### ⚠️ DATE AVAILABILITY VERIFICATION PROTOCOL (TWO-FOLD CHECK)

**This is the #1 most important query the Sales Brain will handle. Accuracy is non-negotiable.**
**The Brain provides data → the sales team confirms before telling any client.**

#### CHECK 1: Primary Calendar Query
- Query the venue's Google Calendar for the requested date(s)
- Pull ALL events for each date in the range

#### CHECK 2: Context Window Query (±1 day)
- Query the day BEFORE and day AFTER each date in the range
- Why: catches multi-day events (ECD wedding weekends with Friday lodging), kitchen prep days that signal venue is in use, and setup/teardown blocks
- For ECD: specifically look for `+M` (Mansion) and `+GH` (Gatehouse) flags on adjacent days — a Friday `ECD+M` wedding means Saturday morning the mansion is still occupied
- For TBB: check if adjacent-day events share floor prefixes that could indicate multi-day corporate bookings

#### RESULT INTERPRETATION
- **No events on date AND no spanning events from adjacent days → OPEN**
- **Event exists → check space prefix to determine what's booked:**
  - TBB prefixes: BBC (Cumberland), BBD (Dyer), BBO (Observatory), BBC+O, BBD+O, BBC+D, TBB (Entire)
  - TBT: Always entire venue
  - ECD: Always entire venue (+M = Mansion lodging, +GH = Gatehouses)
  - SWF: Always entire venue
- **TBB is the only multi-space venue** — a BBC booking leaves Dyer and Observatory potentially open. But if the event name includes `*ALL DAY*` or references lounges, check which lounges are in use (2nd floor = Cumberland package, 5th floor = D+O package)
- **Partial-day bookings:** If only morning or evening is booked, note it but do NOT declare the other half "available" — flag for sales team to assess whether a split-day is feasible

#### SAFETY RULES
1. **NEVER tell a client a date is available** — only provide data to the sales team, who confirms and communicates
2. If an event name doesn't follow naming convention → flag as "unrecognized format, verify manually"
